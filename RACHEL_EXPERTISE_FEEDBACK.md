### 2. Institutional Feeling (Monochromatic + Cold)
✅ **Perfect diagnosis of Graydar + Lower Luxury.**

### 4. Welcomed vs. Trapped (The Nervous System Framework)
✅ **This is gold.** Parasympathetic vs. amygdala triggers are real neuroscience.

**Welcomed State (Parasympathetic):**
- Landing Strip (1-meter clear radius)
- Beacon Effect (warm 2700K light visible from door)
- Visual Transparency (see essentials without opening cupboards)

**Trapped State (Amygdala):**
- Furniture Maze (zigzag path to bathroom)
- Host's Ghost (personal items, fragile things, "Do Not" signs)
- One-Way Room (wall-hugger bed forcing partner climb-over)

**These are DETECTABLE.** The app can flag these from photos.

### 2. The Beacon Effect
**Current:** "Warm lighting visible from doorway"  
**Could add:**
- What's the psychological effect if NO lighting is visible? (Darkness = danger)
- What if ONLY harsh overhead light? (Institutional, not welcoming)
- Photo detection: Can you see a warm light source from the main entry photo?
- **Hedy Logic:** Harsh overhead-only light in entry → flag as "Cold Core" → suggest table lamps

---

### 3. Visual Transparency
**Current:** "See where essentials are without opening cupboards"  
**Could add:**
- Essential locations: Coffee station, towels, remotes, toiletries, charging cables
- What's the psychology of hidden clutter? (Anxiety that guest can't find things)
- Open shelving psychology vs. closed cabinets
- **Example:** Towel basket visible on bathroom counter = welcomed; towels hidden in cabinet = guest hunts for things

---

### 4. Furniture-to-Floor Ratio
**Current:** "If 70% furniture, guest feels physically trapped"  
**Could add:**
- Specific thresholds: 
  - <40% furniture = spacious (positive)
  - 40–60% furniture = normal (neutral)
  - 60–80% furniture = crowded (negative)
  - 80%+ furniture = claustrophobic (very negative)
- Photo detection: Estimate floor visibility in room photos
- **Hedy Logic:** 80%+ furniture → −3 points Clutter dimension

---

### 5. Visual Points of Contention
**Current:** "Personal items, fragile things, Do Not signs"  
**Could add:**
- Count categories:
  - Family photos (especially with strangers' faces)
  - Delicate items on low tables
  - "Do Not" signs
  - Valuable-looking items that look "off-limits"
  - Religious items (can feel personal/intrusive)
- Psychology: Makes guest feel like "clumsy intruder" not "welcomed visitor"
- **Hedy Logic:** 5+ visual points of contention → flag as "Host's Ghost Risk" → recommend "curate personal items"

## Questions to Refine Further

### 1. **The Warm Anchor Point**
You mention "absence of warm anchor prevents rest mode." Can you elaborate?
- What IS a warm anchor? (Brass lamp, wood furniture, warm textile, plants?)
- Why does one warm element in an all-grey room change the psychology?
- Threshold: How much warmth is needed to prevent institutional feeling?

**Example:** Graydar is all grey. It has minimal warm elements. If we added brass lamp + warm throw blanket, would 50% of the institutional feeling go away? 75%?

---

### 2. **The Curated vs. Found Threshold**
You mention "common thread" creates intentionality.
- What are the common threads?
  - Brass hardware (hardware match)
  - Wood tone (material match)
  - Era (all mid-century, all modern, all vintage)
  - Colour family (all earth tones, all jewel tones)
- Can a room have ZERO common thread and still feel curated? (Eclectic vs. chaotic)

**Example:** Delta Blue is chaotic. Rent Free is "found items." What's the dividing line?

---

### 3. **Personal Items Threshold**
You mention "high density of personal items = guest feels like clumsy intruder."
- Is a family photo of 3 okay? Okay? Or does ANY family photo trigger "Host's Ghost"?
- What about travel photos (scenic, no people)?
- What about artwork (abstract, not personal)?
- Should the rule change by room? (Family photos okay in living room, NOT in bedroom?)

**Threshold idea:** 
- Living room: Up to 3 family photos is "welcoming host vibe"
- Bedroom: Zero family photos (creates "Guest's Space" not "Host's Bedroom")
- Bathroom: Zero personal items (guest privacy zone)
