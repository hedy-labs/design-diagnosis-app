## PROFESSIONALISM WALL: Hard Rules for Client-Facing Reports

### Rule 1: Silence Internal Instructions
**What NOT to include in client-facing reports:**
- ❌ "CRITICAL RULE: Do NOT..."
- ❌ "WRONG example... RIGHT example..."
- ❌ "Instructional Logic" or "Internal Framework"
- ❌ Any visible "rules" or "processes"
- ❌ "This is how we analyze" explanations

**What TO include:**
- ✅ Opportunity framing ("Your centered sofa offers an opportunity...")
- ✅ Strategic guidance ("Consider adding...")
- ✅ Professional consulting tone
- ✅ External perspective, not internal logic

**Example Correction:**
```
❌ WRONG: "CRITICAL RULE: Do NOT recommend gallery walls for floating furniture"
✅ RIGHT: "Your centered sofa positioning offers an opportunity to create 
          intentional visual grounding. By adding an 8x10 area rug and 
          positioning a console table strategically, you can enhance the 
          sense of curated design."
```

## VISION DETECTION ACCURACY: Small Items Acknowledgment

### Rule 2: Don't Overstate Absence
**What NOT to say:**
- ❌ "Relies ENTIRELY on overhead lighting"
- ❌ "ZERO USB ports"
- ❌ "No lamps visible"
- ❌ Absolute statements that ignore existing elements

**What TO say:**
- ✅ "Bedside lamps are modest in scale; upgrading to statement-style lamps will improve..."
- ✅ "Modern guests expect convenient charging access; consider adding..."
- ✅ "Current solutions are functional; enhancement opportunities exist..."

**Example Correction:**
```
❌ WRONG: "Your bedroom relies ENTIRELY on recessed overhead lighting"
✅ RIGHT: "Your bedroom includes overhead recessed lighting plus bedside 
          table lamps. However, the bedside lamps are relatively small in 
          scale, which limits their visual presence. Upgrading to larger, 
          statement-style lamps will immediately shift perceived quality."
```

---

## GENERALIZE FINISH RECOMMENDATIONS: Protect Consultation Value

### Rule 3: No Specific Color Prescriptions
**What NOT to include:**
- ❌ "Navy, rust, or sage green"
- ❌ "Warm gray with navy accent"
- ❌ "Specific brand/finish" recommendations
- ❌ Over-prescribed color palettes

**What TO include:**
- ✅ "Colors of your choice"
- ✅ "Pattern or warm tone"
- ✅ "Functional upgrade category" (throw, pillows, rug)
- ✅ "Your preferred aesthetic direction"

**Rationale:** Specific color recommendations belong in consultation, not the report. This preserves the value of one-on-one guidance while providing actionable direction.

**Example Correction:**
```
❌ WRONG: "Add 2-3 accent pillows: navy, rust, or sage green"
✅ RIGHT: "Add 2-3 accent pillows in colors of your choice for visual depth"
```

---

## REMOVE SPECIFIC DOLLAR PROJECTIONS: The ROI Shield

### Rule 4: No Quantified Financial Forecasts
**What NOT to include:**
- ❌ "-$450-750/month revenue loss"
- ❌ "+$290-410/month revenue increase"
- ❌ Specific dollar amount projections
- ❌ "You're losing $X per night"

**What TO include:**
- ✅ "Significantly increase perceived listing value"
- ✅ "Improve competitive positioning in your local market"
- ✅ "Drive increased booking appeal"
- ✅ "Enhance guest satisfaction metrics"

**Rationale:** Specific projections require context (booking rate, base rate, market data) that belongs in consultation. The report should establish value and urgency without making specific financial claims.

**Example Correction:**
```
❌ WRONG: "Monochromatic design costs approximately $450-750 per month 
          in lost bookings"
✅ RIGHT: "The minimalist black/white/gray palette can read as austere 
          rather than warm. Strategic textile enhancements will position 
          your property to capture guests seeking warmth and intentionality."
```

## FUNCTIONAL LOGIC ACCURACY: No Overwrites

### Rule 5: Preserve What Works
**Duvet Example:**
- ❌ Don't say: "Replace duvet with cream or soft white"
- ✅ Say: "Your bedding is acceptable. Layer with textured throw and accent pillows."

**Entry Example:**
- ❌ Don't say: "Add console table to entryway"
- ✅ Say: "A slim vertical shoe rack and elegant wall hooks will preserve your landing space while adding functional style."

**Nightstand Example:**
- ❌ Don't say: "Replace nightstands with larger tables"
- ✅ Say: "Consider nightstands with slightly larger surface area to accommodate phones and reading materials."

## TONE TRANSFORMATION: Professional Consulting

### Rule 6: Consulting Tone (Not Diagnostic)

**Old (Diagnostic) Tone:**
> "Your property is entirely black/white/gray. While 'minimalist,' this palette reads as sterile, institutional, and cold. -5 points from vitality score. Monthly revenue loss: -$450-750."

**New (Consulting) Tone:**
> "Your property features a minimalist black/white/gray palette. While clean and modern, this monochromatic approach can read as austere rather than warm. With strategic textile enhancements, you can balance design sophistication with sensory comfort."

**Differences:**
- Diagnostic = measurement focus ("This scores X, costs Y")
- Consulting = opportunity focus ("This offers a chance to...")
- Diagnostic = rules-based ("You must/shouldn't...")
- Consulting = guidance-based ("Consider... You might want to...")

---

## FIVE PILLARS IN REPORTS: Framework, Not Labels

### Rule 7: Present Pillars as Narrative, Not Metrics

**What the report includes:**
- ✅ Five-part structure (Spatial, Lighting, Sensory, Power, Intentionality)
- ✅ Depth on each dimension
- ✅ Opportunities and strengths
- ✅ Professional framing

**What the report AVOIDS:**
- ❌ Visible pillar scoring system
- ❌ "Pillar 1, Pillar 2, Pillar 3" as labels
- ❌ Raw metric presentation
- ❌ "This pillar scored X/25"

**Integration:** Pillars are the framework structure; reports present findings without exposing the framework.

---

## PROFESSIONAL BOUNDARIES: What's for Consultation

### Items That Belong in Consultation (Not Reports)

1. **Specific Color Palettes** — Report suggests warmth; consultation specifies colors
2. **Brand/Product Recommendations** — Report suggests category; consultation specifies brands
3. **Detailed Budget Breakdown** — Report notes investment tier; consultation details line items
4. **Financial Projections** — Report frames opportunity; consultation quantifies ROI
5. **Phasing Strategy** — Report outlines priorities; consultation sequences implementation
6. **Design Direction** — Report frames options; consultation provides personalized guidance

Before finalizing any premium report:

- [ ] **Professionalism Wall:** No internal rules visible
- [ ] **Vision Accuracy:** Small items acknowledged, not overstated

- [ ] **Financial Projections:** Removed (framed as opportunities instead)
- [ ] **Functional Logic:** Preserved existing value

- [ ] **Consultation Value:** Preserved (not over-prescribed)

- [ ] **High-End:** Professional design consulting tone throughout
