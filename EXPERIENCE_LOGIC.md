# EXPERIENCE_LOGIC.md — Stager's Logic Framework

The scoring engine behind Vitality Score. Documents the design principles that separate a "guest-ready" listing from a mediocre one.

## Five Scoring Dimensions (0–20 each = 100 total)

### 1. Colour Coherence (0–20)
**Does the listing feel intentional, or like a random assembly?**

**Scoring:**
- **18–20:** Cohesive palette across all rooms. Warm OR cool tones consistently applied. Intentional colour blocking (e.g., navy + charcoal + white).
- **15–17:** Mostly cohesive. One or two minor clashes (e.g., warm wood mixed with cool greys, but a unifying element like white/cream bridges them).
- **12–14:** Mixed but acceptable. Warm and cool tones present, but rooms feel separate rather than chaotic.
- **8–11:** Noticeable clashes. Bedroom is warm, bathroom is cool, living room is neutral — no visual throughline.
- **4–7:** Multiple clashing colours without unifying elements. Feels like a furniture rental depot.
- **1–3:** Chaotic. Every surface a different colour. No evidence of intentional design.
- **0:** Actively hostile colour choices (e.g., hot pink wall + orange carpet).

**How to Fix:**
- Pick a primary palette (warm wood OR cool tones, not both)
- Use white, cream, or grey as unifying anchors
- Add colour through removable items (throw pillows, art, plants) not walls/large furniture
- Ensure bedside tables, TV console, and sofa are all in same colour family

**Red Flags:**
- Mismatched wood tones (warm oak bed + cool grey TV console)
- Clashing wall colours between rooms visible from entry
- Competing accent colours with no neutral anchor

### 2. Lighting Quality (0–20)
**Can guests read, relax, and get ready comfortably?**

**Scoring:**
- **18–20:** Multiple light sources per room. Bedside lamps (both sides), overhead + floor/accent lights in living areas. Natural light + supplemental lighting. Warm colour temperature (2700K) preferred.
- **15–17:** Good coverage. Bedside lamp on one side, overhead, occasional floor light. Mostly warm tone.
- **12–14:** Basic. Overhead light + one or two supplemental sources. Some colour temperature variation.
- **8–11:** Minimal. Single harsh overhead light, maybe one lamp. No task lighting (bedside, bathroom vanity, desk).
- **4–7:** Poor. Dark rooms relying on natural light only. No bedside lamps. Harsh overhead.
- **1–3:** Very dark. Single bare bulb or low-watt overhead. Impossible to read or get ready.
- **0:** No lighting documented or obvious safety hazard (broken fixtures, exposed wiring).

**How to Fix:**
- Add bedside lamps (warm tone, 40–60W equivalent)
- Add floor lamp or accent lighting in living areas
- Replace harsh white bulbs with warm white (2700K)
- Ensure bathroom has vanity mirror lighting or strong supplemental light

**Red Flags:**
- Bedroom has no bedside lamps
- Single harsh overhead light in main living area
- Visible colour temperature differences between rooms (cool + warm bulbs in same space)

---

### 3. Functional Anchors (0–20)
**Can guests actually sleep, work, and dine comfortably?**

Essential items:
- **Bedroom:** Pair of bedside tables (not just one), pair of matching lamps, bed appears made with quality linens
- **Living/Dining:** Dining table with seating for guest count, sofa side tables (for drinks/remotes), adequate seating
- **Entry:** Hooks for coats/bags, shoe storage or bench, clear space to set down luggage
- **Workspace (if applicable):** Dedicated desk + proper chair (not kitchen stool or vanity chair)
- **Bathroom:** Shower shelf/caddy, hooks, place to set toiletries

**Scoring:**
- **18–20:** All anchors present. Matched pairs (bedside tables, lamps). Quality finishes.
- **15–17:** Most anchors present. One or two missing or mismatched.
- **12–14:** Key anchors present but incomplete (e.g., one bedside table, no workspace).
- **8–11:** Several missing. Bedroom has no bedside tables, no entry storage, no sofa side tables.
- **4–7:** Major gaps. No dining table, no workspace, minimal entry storage, single bedside table.
- **1–3:** Severe. Bed has no tables/lamps, no dining setup, no entry organization, no workspace.
- **0:** Unusable space. No seating, no dining, dangerous/broken furniture.

**How to Fix:**
- **Highest impact:** Add pair of bedside tables + lamps (single-biggest bang for buck)
- Add sofa side table for drinks/remotes
- Add entry hooks + slim shoe rack or bench
- Add workspace (desk + proper chair) if room allows
- For small studios: use console tables to create functional zones

**Red Flags:**
- Single bedside table (not a pair)
- No bedside lamps or only one lamp
- Sofa with nowhere to set drinks
- No dining table (or undersized table for guest count)
- No entry storage — guests have nowhere to put shoes/bags
- Workspace missing in urban studio or city apartment

### 4. Clutter & Space Flow (0–20)
**Does the space feel spacious and breathable, or cramped and messy?**

**Scoring:**
- **18–20:** Clear floor space, strategic furniture placement, zones defined in open-plan layouts, no visible personal items, clutter-free surfaces
- **15–17:** Mostly clear. Some floor space, minimal clutter, zones feel defined.
- **12–14:** Functional but busy. Some clutter visible, furniture scaled appropriately but spacing tight.
- **8–11:** Cluttered. Too much furniture for room size, visible clutter, personal items visible, narrow pathways.
- **4–7:** Very cluttered. Crowded furniture, piled items, unclear zones, feels cramped.
- **1–3:** Chaotic. Barely navigable, heavy personal clutter, furniture completely dominates space.
- **0:** Safety hazard. Blocked exits, unstable stacks, unsafe layout.

**How to Fix (Listing-Type Specific):**
- **Urban Studio / City Apartment (CRITICAL):**
  - Wall-mount TV if on console (reclaims floor space)
  - Reorient sofa so back faces bed zone, creating visual barrier
  - Use console table behind sofa as divider
  - Remove unnecessary furniture (side tables that don't serve functional anchor role)
  - Rugs to define zones
- **Resort / Family Home:**
  - Ensure dining area is uncluttered
  - Clear pathways to all rooms
  - Storage for guest items (luggage, beach gear)
- **General:**
  - Minimize visible personal items
  - One clear pathway through each room
  - Surfaces clear except décor/staging props

**Red Flags:**
- TV on large console (wastes floor space) — should be wall-mounted
- Sofa and bed facing same direction with no visual divider
- Narrow pathways between furniture
- Visible clutter (laundry, personal items, equipment)
- Furniture scaled too large for room

### 5. Staging Integrity (0–20)
**Does what the guest sees match what they'll find? Are staging props honest?**

**What to PHOTOGRAPH (every photo is a promise):**
- Kitchens (must show: fridge, cooktop, sink, counter space)
- Bathrooms (must show: shower/tub, toilet, vanity, mirror, towel racks)
- Bedrooms (must show: bed, bedside tables/lamps, wardrobe/closet)
- Dining areas (must show: dining table, chairs, place settings if set)
- Living areas (must show: sofa, TV, seating, side tables)
- Entry (must show: hooks, shoe storage, entry mat, clear space)

**What to MENTION ONLY (never stage/photograph):**
- Vacuum, mop, cleaning supplies, iron, washing machine
- Owner personal items, religious items, decorative non-functional items

**Staging Props (MUST be provided to all guests):**
- Wine, fruit, chocolates, flowers on surfaces ✅ Only if you provide these to every guest
- Throw blankets ✅ Only if available for guest use
- Welcome flowers ✅ Only if you refresh them for every check-in

**Staging Props (MUST NOT photograph):**
- Food/drink not actually provided (immediate review bomb)
- Towel art (confusing for guests)
- Hand-printed signs or chalkboards (looks amateur)
- TV showing curated content or streaming (looks fake; should be off or showing home screen)

**Mirror/Reflection Hazards:**
- If bedroom mirror reflects unmade bed or personal items, angle or cover
- If bathroom mirror reflects clutter, clear before shooting
- Kitchen window reflections showing unstaged side of house = flag

**Scoring:**
- **18–20:** All required rooms shown, no lying staging props, surfaces tidy, mirrors/reflections clear, props provided to guests
- **15–17:** All required rooms shown, minimal questionable props, mostly honest
- **12–14:** Most rooms shown, one or two gaps (e.g., no dining photo), some props slightly questionable
- **8–11:** Missing key room (no bathroom photo, no dining shown), staging props not clearly provided, some mirror issues
- **4–7:** Multiple missing rooms, significant prop fraud (wine/flowers not provided), misleading reflections
- **1–3:** Major omissions, heavy prop fraud, actively deceptive
- **0:** Safety/health hazard (mould visible but not mentioned, broken fixtures not disclosed)

**How to Fix:**
- Photograph every required room
- Remove staging props you don't provide, or
- Update Airbnb to note: "Welcome basket includes wine and fruit"
- Clear mirror reflections before shooting
- If props (flowers, wine) are in photo, commit to providing them

**Red Flags:**
- Beautiful wine bottle on bedside table but not mentioned in amenities
- Flowers in every photo but note says "no fresh flowers"
- Bathroom not photographed (huge red flag)
- Mirror reflection showing messy second room
- TV showing Netflix/streaming (should be off or home screen)
- Dining table not shown despite being in property

## Reality Gap Score (Optional, 0–80 if guest report provided)

If a guest complains after arrival, score the gap between photos and reality:

**Categories:**
1. **Mould & Hygiene** (0–10)
   - 0 = None reported
   - 3 = Minor (small stain, questionable smell)
   - 6 = Moderate (visible mould, bathroom issues)
   - 10 = Severe (health hazard)

2. **Plumbing Integrity** (0–10)
   - 0 = None
   - 3 = Minor (weak pressure, occasional drips)
   - 6 = Moderate (backed-up drain, no hot water)
   - 10 = Non-functional

3. **Pest Presence** (0–10)
   - 0 = None
   - 3 = Evidence (droppings, webs)
   - 6 = Active (seen insects/rodents)
   - 10 = Infestation

4. **Electrical & Fixtures** (0–10)
   - Broken lights, outlets, switches, appliances

5. **Furniture Safety** (0–10)
   - Broken bed frame, unstable chairs, sharp edges

6. **Linen & Bedding Quality** (0–10)
   - Pilling, stains, thin, wrong thread count

7. **Props Fraud** (0–10)
   - Wine/flowers not provided, amenities misrepresented

8. **Host Responsiveness** (0–10)
   - Host ignored guest message, didn't fix issues

**Severity Scale:**
- 0–25: Minor (easily fixed, unlikely to leave bad review)
- 26–45: Moderate (guest likely to complain publicly)
- 46–65: Serious (major discrepancy, review bomb likely)
- 66–80: Fraudulent (deceptive listing, legal/dispute territory)

## Listing Type Weights

### Urban Studio (Highest Functional Anchor Weight)
- Guests live/work/sleep in ONE room
- Workspace = CRITICAL (desk + chair)
- Entry organization = HIGH (tiny space makes clutter worse)
- Colour Coherence = HIGH (small space unforgiving of clashing colours)
- **Weight adjustments:** Functional Anchors +2, Colour Coherence +1, Clutter -1

### City Apartment (Clutter + Functional Anchor Weight)
- Mix of living/bedroom zones in limited space
- TV wall-mounting = HIGH PRIORITY (reclaims floor space)
- Sofa orientation = CRITICAL (back facing bed = visual barrier)
- Workspace = IMPORTANT (for bleisure guests)
- **Weight adjustments:** Clutter +1, Functional Anchors +1

### Resort / Villa (Colour Coherence + Lighting Weight)
- Guests expect curated aesthetic
- Outdoor areas visible in photos = important context
- Lighting quality (ambience) = CRITICAL
- Staging integrity for tropical/local design = HIGH
- **Weight adjustments:** Colour Coherence +1, Lighting +1, Staging +1

### Family Home (Functional Anchors + Safety Weight)
- Multiple sleeping zones, dining for 4+, storage for family gear
- Furniture safety = CRITICAL (kids use it)
- Functional Anchors (multiple beds, dining, workspace) = CRITICAL
- Clutter = CRITICAL (safety hazard)
- **Weight adjustments:** Functional Anchors +1, Staging -1 (less need for aspirational props)

## Design Philosophy

> **"Would you live here? Your guests have to."**

Every recommendation is grounded in one question: **What would make this space feel intentional, comfortable, and trustworthy to a guest arriving for the first time?**

- Colour coherence isn't about "perfect taste" — it's about intentionality. Guests trust hosts who make deliberate choices.
- Functional anchors aren't about luxury — they're about usability. A bedside lamp tells a guest: "This host thought about my needs."
- Staging integrity isn't about honesty police — it's about trust. A guest who finds exactly what they expect leaves a 5-star review.

Good staging is invisible. It's not aspirational; it's contextual. It shows the space as it will be experienced.
