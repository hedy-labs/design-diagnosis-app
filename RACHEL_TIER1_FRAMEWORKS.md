## FRAMEWORK #2: Sovereignty vs. Surveillance

### Core Concept
Guests feel either **welcomed** (sovereignty — freedom to use space) or **trapped** (surveillance — too many rules, fragile items, visual noise).

### SOVEREIGNTY (WELCOMED)

#### The Path of Least Resistance
- **Clear floor space:** Can guest navigate without moving furniture?
- **Inviting lighting:** Is there warm light visible from entry? (Beacon effect)
- **Permission signals:** Empty hooks, clear surfaces, "you can use this" vibe
- **Open storage:** Guest can see where things are (transparency)

**Photo Detection Logic:**
- Floor visible in entry photo? ✅ (Path of Least Resistance)
- Hooks empty/available? ✅
- Surfaces clear? ✅
- Warm light visible? ✅

**Scoring:** Each element present = +1 point → Max 4 points for Sovereignty score

#### The Escape Route (Critical for Bedrooms)
- **Buffer zone:** 50cm minimum on BOTH sides of bed
- **If not present:** −10 points from Vitality Score (severe claustrophobia trigger)
- **Why it matters:** Psychological freedom = safety = rest = good reviews

**Photo Detection Logic:**
- Can you see bed clearly from entry?
- Is there visible floor space on both sides?
- Can guest move freely without climbing over partner?

---

### SURVEILLANCE (TRAPPED)

#### Host-Heavy Decor
- Too many rules ("Do Not Touch," "Do Not Sit," warning signs)
- Overly fragile items (delicate crystal, precious decorations)
- "Visual Noise" (busy patterns, stripes, conflicting colours)
- Personal items creating "clumsy intruder" feeling

**Scoring:** Count visual stress triggers
- Rule signs present? −2
- Fragile items on low tables? −1 per item
- Busy patterns dominating? −3
- Family photos in bedroom? −1

**Hedy Logic:** If total Surveillance score >5 points, flag as "Host-Heavy Risk"

---

### THE TWO MINDSETS

**SOVEREIGNTY MINDSET:** "This is my space to use freely" → Guest relaxes → Better reviews → Higher bookings

**SURVEILLANCE MINDSET:** "I'm being watched, I might break something" → Guest anxious → Subconscious 3-star → Lower repeat bookings

## FRAMEWORK #3: Price-Adjusted Vitality

### The Core Math

**A Vitality Score means NOTHING without price context.**

Example:
- **Free Will:** 53/100 at $60/night = SUCCESS (guest expectation = clean + bed)
- **Rent Free:** 53/100 at $200/night = DISASTER (guest expectation = luxury + wow factor)

**Same score. Opposite outcomes.** Why? Expectation anchoring.

---

### THE FOUR PRICE TIERS

#### Tier 1: $0–$80/Night (The "Free Will" Strategy)
**Guest Mindset:** "I just need a clean place to crash."

**What Justifies This Price:**
- Clean bed + functional bathroom = 50/100 acceptable
- WiFi working + shower hot water = success
- Free parking OR great location = tier up

**Tolerance:** HIGH. Guest excuses:
- "Furniture is basic" ✓ (expected at this price)
- "Decor is simple" ✓ (expected at this price)
- "Found items" ✓ (expected at this price)

**53/100 Score = VICTORY** (exceeds expectations)

**Upgrade Path:** None needed yet. Maintain cleanliness, add WiFi speed, that's it.

---

#### Tier 2: $80–$150/Night (The "Graydar" Strategy)
**Guest Mindset:** "I want comfort + some design polish."

**What Justifies This Price:**
- Comfortable furniture (not basic)
- Intentional design (some curation)
- Amenities (WiFi, coffee station, workspace)
- Lighting that works (no harsh overhead)

**Tolerance:** MODERATE. Guest excuses:
- "Furniture is mismatched" ✗ (no excuse at this price)
- "Bed is uncomfortable" ✗ (deal-breaker)
- "Decor feels institutional" ✗ (deal-breaker)

**53/100 Score = FAILURE** (doesn't meet expectation)  
**65–72/100 Score = SUCCESS** (meets/exceeds expectation)

**Upgrade Path (Example: Graydar at $125):**
- Add warm accent colours (throws, pillows) → +3–5 points
- Add coffee table + side tables → +2–3 points
- Add soft lighting (lamps) → +2–3 points
- Result: 69 → 78/100 → justify price increase to $145–165

**The Fix Logic:**
- $50 rug = $5/night price increase justified (breaks even in 10 months)
- $200 mattress topper = $10/night increase justified ("Best sleep ever" becomes marketing hook)
- $300 paint job = $15/night increase justified (refresh entire vibe)

---

#### Tier 3: $150–$250/Night (The "Hobby Lobby" Strategy)
**Guest Mindset:** "I am paying for an experience/identity. Wow me."

**What Justifies This Price:**
- Premium finishes (quality linens, furniture, lighting)
- Professional design (curated, intentional, cohesive)
- Luxury amenities (premium coffee, curated snacks, fresh flowers)
- Unique features (fireplace, hot tub, views)
- Immaculate condition (no wear, no stains, pristine)

**Tolerance:** ZERO. Guest excuses:
- "Plates don't match" ✗ (instant 1-star: "cheap vibes")
- "Linens are worn" ✗ (instant 1-star: "low quality")
- "Decor feels budget" ✗ (instant 1-star: "not worth $200")

**73/100 Score = FAILURE** (doesn't meet expectation)  
**85–93/100 Score = SUCCESS** (meets/exceeds expectation)

**Upgrade Path (Example: Lower Luxury at $186):**
- Currently: 56/100 (structural underperformance)
- Option A: Invest $5K–7K to reach 80+/100, justify $200 price
- Option B: Reposition to $120/night, be successful at that tier
- Rachel's recommendation: Option B (pricing error too severe for quick fix)

**The Fix Logic:** Premium pricing requires premium execution. There's no "good enough" at $200.

---

#### Tier 4: $250+/Night (The "Luxury Suite" Strategy)
**Guest Mindset:** "This is an investment in a luxury experience."

**What Justifies This Price:**
- Bespoke design (commissioned furniture, curated art)
- High-end finishes (marble, designer lighting, luxury linens)
- Exclusive amenities (wine cellar, smart home, private spa)
- Immaculate + new condition
- Unique story ("historic home," "architect-designed," "celebrity owned")

**Tolerance:** ZERO. This is luxury — any flaw is inexcusable.

**90+/100 Score = MINIMUM EXPECTATION** (anything below feels like betrayal)

**Upgrade Path:** Not for Design Diagnosis MVP. This is future tier.
