## Video Shooting Tips

### Setup
- **Phone:** Use your main camera (back camera is better quality)
- **Location:** Quiet room, no echoes (bedroom is fine)
- **Lighting:** Natural light preferred, avoid harsh shadows
- **Background:** Anything is fine (you're recording voiceover, not on camera)

### CapCut Workflow
1. Add photos/screenshots to timeline
2. Add royalty-free music (CapCut library)
3. Add text overlays (titles, key points, hashtags)
4. Record voiceover directly in CapCut (or import pre-recorded)
5. Export as MP4 (1080×1920 for TikTok vertical)

### Voiceover Tips
- **Pace:** Slow enough to understand, fast enough to hold attention
- **Tone:** Warm, helpful, conversational (not robotic)
- **Volume:** Consistent (test before recording all 10)
- **No background noise:** Record in quiet space
- **Do multiple takes:** Pick the best one

### Example Voiceover (Video 1)
```
"This $88 a night Airbnb looks beautiful, right? 
So our AI scored it. And... yeah. Thirty-eight out of one hundred. 
Here's why. Beautiful bed, but NO bedside lamps. 
Guests can't read. Entry is bare — no hooks, no shoe storage. 
Sofa's got nowhere for drinks. Three simple fixes and this jumps to a sixty-seven. 
Want to score your own listing free? Link in bio."
```

Total time: ~30 seconds, conversational, includes problem + solution + CTA.

## Red Flags to Avoid

❌ **Don't:**
- Post inconsistently (week on, week off = algorithm kills you)
- Make voiceovers too salesy (turn off hosts)
- Use other people's Airbnbs without anonymizing
- Forget to link to landing page (lost traffic)
- Overcomplicate the free tool (keep it 2 minutes)

✅ **Do:**
- Post 2/day, same times (let algorithm learn your schedule)
- Be educational + entertaining (roast + fix, not just roast)
- Anonymize listings ("This $88/night property" not "This specific listing")
- Make link-in-bio obvious (say it every video)
- Make tool dead simple (upload photos → get score → see shopping list)
