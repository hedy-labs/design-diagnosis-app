# SCRIPT 10: BONUS — The Hidden Friction Audit (What Guests Feel But Don't Say)

**Focus:** Teach "Sovereignty vs. Surveillance" framework  
**Length:** ~85 seconds

---

## [VISUALS]
- Open with generic Airbnb photos
- Zoom in on subtle design details (rule signs, fragile items, crowded surfaces)
- Show before/after (cluttered entry vs. clean entry with hooks and bench)
- Design Diagnosis report highlighting "Sovereignty" score

- Conclusion: show same property, different design, different ratings

---

## [HOOK IDEA]
"Guests don't consciously think about this. But they *feel* it. And it affects their rating."

---

## [VOICEOVER]

"I want to teach you something that separates expert designers from everyone else.

[Pause]

It's not about whether your sofa is cute. It's about whether guests feel *welcomed* or *watched.*

[Transition]

When a guest walks into your rental, there's an instant feeling: 'Am I allowed to use this space? Or am I going to break something?'

[Point out the details]

If your entry has:
- Clear floor space ✓
- Empty hooks for coats ✓
- Available surfaces ✓
- Warm lighting ✓

→ Guest feels: 'I can relax here.'

[But if your entry has]

- Rule signs everywhere ✗
- Fragile items on low tables ✗

That anxiety? It translates directly to review scores. Same property, same cleanliness, but different *feeling.*

[Final beat]
